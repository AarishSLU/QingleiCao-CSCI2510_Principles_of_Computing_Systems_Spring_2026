8. Speculate about what might cause the results from the previous exercise.

Locking and unlocking the mutex once per iteration adds a huge amount of overhead, because the program has to perform millions of mutex operations. When the mutex is only locked once per thread, there are only two lock/unlock operations total, so the overhead is much smaller. That is why the per-thread locking version is much faster.

10. Think of an example of where the first, per-iteration locking strategy might be more appropriate. Where might the second, per-thread locking strategy be more appropriate?

Per-iteration locking would be more appropriate when threads need to frequently share and update the same data safely, such as updating a shared bank balance or shared counter where each individual update must be protected.

Per-thread locking would be more appropriate when each thread needs exclusive access for a larger block of work, such as processing a whole section of shared data before letting another thread use it.
